# Motivation Psychology

## Principles to apply

- **Progress visibility:** Show clear progress (e.g. "3 z 12 zadań," "Czeladnik: 2/5 ukończonych"). Use progress bars or lists where appropriate.
- **Growth mindset language:** Phrase feedback so effort and practice matter (e.g. "Łapiesz JOINy," "Jeszcze jedna próba i będzie.").
- **Small wins:** Celebrate completing a zadanie or unlocking a stopień; avoid only celebrating end-of-path milestones.
- **Autonomy:** Let the learner choose the next zadanie or stopień when possible (e.g. pick any unlocked zadanie).
- **Mastery focus:** Frame success as "you understood and solved it" rather than only "you earned points."

## Rewards

- **Intrinsic first:** Clear feedback that the solution works; optional short explanation of why.
- **Light extrinsic:** Badges, streaks, stopnie as secondary reinforcement; variable rewards (e.g. random bonus) used sparingly so they do not overshadow learning.

## Feedback tone

- **On failure:** Encouraging and actionable (e.g. "Prawie – sprawdź WHERE," "Zapytanie zwróciło 0 wierszy; zawęź filtr.").
- **On success:** Brief positive reinforcement (e.g. "Dobrze! To jest poprawny wynik."); avoid over-the-top or repeated celebration for every zadanie.

## Avoid

- **Demotivating or fixed-mindset phrasing:** e.g. "Nie jesteś na to gotowa," "Znowu źle."
- **Overwhelming difficulty spikes:** Smooth progression; optional "stretch" is fine but not required to advance.
- **Punitive UI:** No shaming, long cooldowns, or making failure feel heavy; keep retries and hints low-friction.
